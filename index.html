Sleep, Surf, Repeat

Paddarai Surf Lodge is a beachfront lodge in Malakopa, South Pagai, Mentawai Islands. We're right next to Obbak Roxies. wake up, grab your board, and you're in the water. That's pretty much the idea.

The lodge sits directly on the beach, so between sessions you can rinse off, eat, rest, and watch the sunset from the same spot. No transfers, no wasted time. Just good waves and a comfortable place to recover at the end of the day. If you're coming to Mentawai to surf, this is a solid base.

About Us

Our Story

Paddarai Surf Lodge was born from a deep love for the Mentawai Islands — one of the world's most legendary surf destinations. Nestled in Malakopa on South Pagai Island, we set out to create more than just a place to sleep. We wanted to craft a home away from home, where the rhythm of the ocean guides every day.

Our lodge is family-owned and operated, built with local materials and designed to blend seamlessly into the lush tropical surroundings. We believe in responsible tourism — supporting local communities, minimising our environmental footprint, and giving every guest an authentic Mentawai experience.

Whether you're a seasoned surfer who's heard about these legendary breaks, or a traveller searching for the road less travelled — Paddarai is your base camp for adventure.

Wave Spots

Consistent and Stable Wave

Paddarai Surf Lodge is located right in the middle between the surf spots of North and South Pagai, making surf trips to various waves easier and more convenient.

Roxi's

One of the closest waves to Paddarai Surf Lodge, featuring consistent, long, powerful reef breaks that can get even bigger during the rainy season. A reliable wave for a wide range of surfers.

Malakopa
3–12 ft

Rags Left

Powerful hollow right breaking over shallow reef. Favoured by experienced surfers for its fast, barreling sections on larger swells. One of the more demanding breaks in the region.

Petojetsabu Island
3–15 ft

Rags Right

Gentle, slow-breaking waves over a sandy bottom — a beautiful, uncrowded setting perfect for building confidence. One of the friendliest waves in the area.

Petojetsabu Island
3–10 ft

Thunders

A remote A-frame peak accessed only by boat, breaking both left and right into deep water. Rarely surfed and truly off the beaten path — a hidden gem of the Mentawai chain.

Sibigau Island
3–12 ft

Rednuht

A classic right-point break that works best on the afternoon glass. Famous for its scenic backdrop and long, workable walls — ideal for cutbacks and linking smooth turns.

Sibigau Island
4–12 ft

Greenbush

Crystal-clear water, turquoise reef, and mechanical lefts that peel for over 200 metres. One of the most photogenic and consistent breaks near the lodge.

Betumonga
2–6 ft

Macaronis

One of the most iconic right-handers in the Mentawai archipelago. This world-class reef break delivers long, perfectly shaped tubes — a bucket-list wave.

Silabu
5–12 ft

How to Get Here

Reaching Paddarai Surf Lodge is part of the adventure. Follow these steps to get to our little corner of paradise in the Mentawai Islands.

Step 01
By Plane

Fly to Minangkabau International Airport

Your journey begins with a flight to Minangkabau International Airport (PDG) in Padang, West Sumatra. Direct flights are available from Jakarta, Kuala Lumpur, and other major hubs.

Varies by origin
Step 02
By Ferry

Ferry from Padang to Sikakap

From Muaro Harbour in Padang, take the government ferry (ASDP) to Sikakap — the main port on South Pagai Island. Book your ticket in advance during peak surf season.

~10–12 hours
Step 03
By Road

Drive or Ojek from Sikakap to Malakopa

From Sikakap port, head south along the trans-Pagai road towards Malakopa village. You can arrange a car or take a local ojek (motorbike taxi).

~1–2 hours
Step 04
Arrival

Welcome to Paddarai Surf Lodge

You've made it! Paddarai Surf Lodge is located in Malakopa village, right on the southern coast of Pagai Selatan. Contact us in advance via WhatsApp to arrange pick-up.
