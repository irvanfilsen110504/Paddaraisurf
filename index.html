About Us

Our Story

Paddarai Surf Lodge was born from a deep love for the Mentawai Islands — one of the world's most legendary surf destinations. Nestled in Malakopa on South Pagai Island, we set out to create more than just a place to sleep. We wanted to craft a home away from home, where the rhythm of the ocean guides every day.

Our lodge is family-owned and operated, built with local materials and designed to blend seamlessly into the lush tropical surroundings. We believe in responsible tourism — supporting local communities, minimising our environmental footprint, and giving every guest an authentic Mentawai experience.

Whether you're a seasoned surfer who's heard about these legendary breaks, or a traveller searching for the road less travelled — Paddarai is your base camp for adventure.

Mentawai Archipelago

The Surf

World-Class Breaks at Your Doorstep

The Mentawai Islands are home to some of the most consistent and powerful surf on earth. From Paddarai, you're within boat distance of seven incredible wave spots.

01

Roxi's

One of the closest waves to Paddarai Surf Lodge, featuring consistent, long, powerful reef breaks that can get even bigger during the rainy season. A reliable wave that suits a wide range of surfers.

Malakopa
3–12 ft
02

Rags Left

Powerful hollow right breaking over shallow reef. Favoured by experienced surfers for its fast, barreling sections on larger swells. One of the more demanding breaks in the region.

Petojetsabu Island
3–15 ft
03

Rags Right

Gentle, slow-breaking waves over a sandy bottom — a beautiful, uncrowded setting perfect for building confidence and having fun in the water. One of the friendliest waves in the area.

Petojetsabu Island
3–10 ft
04

Thunders

A remote A-frame peak accessed only by boat, breaking both left and right into deep water. Rarely surfed and truly off the beaten path — a hidden gem of the Mentawai chain.

Sibigau Island
3–12 ft
05

Rednuht

A classic right-point break that works best on the afternoon glass. Famous for its scenic backdrop and long, workable walls — ideal for cutbacks and linking smooth turns down the line.

Sibigau Island
4–12 ft
06

Greenbush

Crystal-clear water, turquoise reef, and mechanical lefts that peel for over 200 metres. One of the most photogenic and consistent breaks near the lodge — a must-surf during any stay.

Betumonga
2–6 ft
07

Macaronis

One of the most iconic right-handers in the Mentawai archipelago. This world-class reef break delivers long, perfectly shaped tubes that challenge even the most seasoned surfers — a bucket-list wave.

Silabu
5–12 ft
